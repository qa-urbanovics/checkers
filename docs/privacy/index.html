Checkers: Emerald Courts

Privacy Policy

June 2026

I built Checkers: Emerald Courts as a solo project — a clean, offline game with no backend, no accounts, and no nonsense. This page explains how the app handles your data. Spoiler: it doesn't collect any.

I'm not a big company with a legal team. I'm a developer who wanted to make a good checkers game. This privacy policy is short because there's genuinely nothing to report.

🔒

What the app stores

The app saves two things to your device — your settings (board theme, piece style, language, sound) and your game stats (wins, losses, total games). That's it. Both live in your phone's local storage and never go anywhere else.

Nothing is sent to a server. There is no server. The app works 100% offline.

📡

No tracking, no analytics

There are no analytics SDKs, no crash reporters, no ad networks, and no third-party trackers of any kind in this app. I don't know how many people play it, how long sessions last, or anything else. I didn't build any of that.

👤

No account required

You don't sign up for anything. There's no login, no profile, no cloud sync. Open the app, play a game, close it. No data about you exists anywhere outside your device.

👶

Safe for everyone

The app is rated 4+. Since nothing is collected at all, it's safe for players of any age. There's nothing to misuse.

🗑️

Deleting your data

To wipe everything — uninstall the app. That removes the local storage. You can also reset your stats inside Settings → Statistics → Reset.

📝

Updates to this page

If I ever add something that changes how data is handled, I'll update this page and bump the date at the top. Given the app is fully offline, that's unlikely.

🌍

GDPR & international users

Since the app collects no personal data whatsoever, there is nothing to process, store, transfer, or delete under GDPR, CCPA, or any other privacy regulation. You have full control — your settings live on your device and can be erased by uninstalling the app.

⚖️

Limitation of liability

The app is provided "as is" without warranties of any kind. I'm not liable for any damages arising from its use. It's a checkers game — the worst thing that can happen is you lose a match.

This policy is governed by the laws of the Republic of Latvia, where the developer is based, without regard to conflict of law principles.
